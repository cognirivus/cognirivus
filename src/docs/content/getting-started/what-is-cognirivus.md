# What is Cognirivus?

**Cognirivus** is a high-density knowledge network built for readers, researchers, and builders who want more than an endless generic feed. It combines source following, curated collections, community workspaces, and real-time discussion so you can build a reading graph around what you actually trust and care about.

Most modern content platforms flatten the web into a stream of standalone posts. Cognirivus does the opposite: it treats **knowledge discovery** as something people actively shape.

---

## Core Philosophy

Cognirivus is built on three main principles:

1. **Source-First Structure**: Instead of only seeing posts shared by individuals, the platform keeps the underlying source graph visible. You follow feeds, websites, and authors directly.
2. **Context Preservation**: When you share a link, it remains anchored to its original source. You can discuss, tag, and organize source items without losing the connection to where they came from.
3. **High Density, No Noise**: The design uses a compact, information-dense layout (inspired by Hacker News and classic reader dashboards) with powerful filtering so you can scan large amounts of information quickly.

---

## Key Concepts

To get the most out of Cognirivus, it helps to understand its three primary layers:

### 1. The Source Graph

The platform regularly ingests content from websites and RSS feeds that you follow. These ingested items are called **Source Items**. They are clean, readable snapshots of articles, blog posts, or media, stored for easy reading and curation.

### 2. Curated Collections

A **Collection** is a reusable reading list. You can save Source Items or custom posts into collections to group them by topic, project, or interest. Collections can be private to you or public to the community.

### 3. Communities

A **Community** is a shared workspace centered around a topic. Communities have their own members, shared feeds, custom collections, and dedicated chat rooms for synchronous discussion.

---

## Why Use Cognirivus?

- **Take Control of Your Information**: Escape recommendation algorithms that optimize for engagement over value. You decide what sources make up your feed.
- **Combine Async & Sync Workflows**: Read articles in your own time, curate them into collections, and discuss them in real-time with others in community chats or DMs.
- **Explore Similar Links**: Use your trusted domain list to discover related reading from "your world" rather than the broader, noisier web.
