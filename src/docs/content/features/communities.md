# Communities

Communities are shared workspaces that bring users together around a specific topic, discipline, or project.

---

## Community Elements

Every community in Cognirivus includes:

1. **Shared Feed**: A stream of posts, source items, and articles shared specifically to this community by its members.
2. **Dedicated Chat**: A real-time room for synchronous discussion. It includes thread replies, reactions, and online presence indicators.
3. **Shared Collections**: Curation folders owned by the community.
4. **Member List**: Shows members, their roles, and who is online.

---

## Membership & Roles

Communities have three user roles:

- **Owner**: Has full control over the community settings, can delete the community, manage billing (if applicable), and appoint admins.
- **Admin**: Can approve/reject member requests, manage collections, moderate posts and chat messages, and edit community details.
- **Member**: Can read the feed, post content, join chat, react to messages, and participate in discussions.

---

## Community Visibility

When creating a community, you can choose its visibility:

- **Public**: Anyone can find the community, see its feed and chat history, and request to join or join instantly.
- **Private**: Hidden from directories. Membership is invitation-only, and content is visible only to accepted members.

---

## Creating a Community

1. Go to **Communities** in the sidebar.
2. Click **Create Community** at the top.
3. Fill out the **Slug** (the unique URL key, like `/c/svelte`), **Name**, and **Description**.
4. Choose the visibility type and click **Create**.
