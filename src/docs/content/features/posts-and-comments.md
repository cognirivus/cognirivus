# Posts & Comments

Discussions in Cognirivus turn raw reading material into active knowledge sharing. You can share your own writing, highlight external articles, or discuss existing source items.

---

## Post Types

When you create a post via the **Submit** page, you can choose from three formats:

1. **Text**: Write original articles or notes in Markdown.
2. **Link**: Share a URL from around the web. If the URL corresponds to an existing followed source, it will link automatically.
3. **Media**: Upload images or rich media, which will render directly in the feed.

---

## Sharing Ingested Items

One of the unique features of Cognirivus is the ability to share a **Source Item** (which was ingested automatically) as a **Post**.

- This creates a discussion thread for that specific item.
- It retains a clean metadata link back to the parent source.
- It alerts other users who follow that source that a discussion is happening.

---

## Threaded Comments

Comments use a nested, hierarchical structure, allowing multiple conversations to develop under a single post without getting cluttered:

- **Collapse Threads**: Click on the vertical line next to a comment to collapse it and all its children.
- **Markdown Support**: You can use standard Markdown inside comments to format text, add lists, or insert code blocks.

---

## Voting & Scoring

Every post and comment can be upvoted or downvoted by members. The **Score** is calculated as:

$$\text{Score} = \text{Likes} - \text{Dislikes}$$

High-scoring posts rise to the top of the **Top** feed. High-scoring comments are sorted first in discussion threads.
