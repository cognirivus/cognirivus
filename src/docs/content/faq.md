# Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to the most common questions about Cognirivus.

---

## General Questions

### What is the difference between a Source and a Post?

- A **Source** represents a publication, feed, or channel that publishes content (e.g., _Svelte Blog_, _Hacker News RSS_, _dbt website_). You follow sources to populate your personal feed.
- A **Post** is created by a user. It can contain original text, an uploaded image, or it can point to a specific **Source Item** to start a discussion thread about it.

### How do I customize my feed?

Go to **Sources** and follow the sites and RSS feeds you want to read. The **For You** tab on the main feed page will then contain exclusively items ingested from those sources. You can also follow curated **Collections** to see items saved by other users.

### Can I follow private RSS feeds?

Currently, Cognirivus only supports public RSS/Atom feeds. We plan to support authenticated or private feed endpoints in a future update.

---

## Curation & Communities

### What is a Curation Note?

When you add an item to a **Collection**, you can add a short note (using Markdown) explaining why it is included, what you learned, or why it's worth reading. This adds context for anyone who follows your collection.

### Who can post in a Community?

Any accepted member of a community can post to its shared feed and join the real-time chat. Admins have additional rights to pin posts, edit details, and manage membership requests.

---

## Common Questions

### How often are sources checked for updates?

Sources are checked for updates automatically throughout the day. If you want to pull new items immediately, you can trigger a manual sync for any source you follow directly from your **Sources** dashboard.
